About

I study the interstellar medium (ISM) in the Milky Way, particularly focusing on the distribution of dust and gas, turbulence, extinction, and the structure of molecular clouds. I utilize and analyze Gaia-based 3D dust-extinction maps to derive density probability distributions (PDFs) and compare 3D and 2D statistics to study nearby molecular clouds in the solar neighborhood. Our goal is to use these spatial 3D dust maps to better understand the physics of molecular clouds and how stars form within them.

Beyond research, I am deeply passionate about outreach and public science. I am particularly interested in philosophy and science, especially astronomy and evolution, and the emergent phenomena in nature, from small scales to large scales. I find meaning in curiosity through science and creativity through arts, including photography, music, and graphic design.
